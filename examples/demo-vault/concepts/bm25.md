# BM25

BM25 (Best Matching 25) is a bag-of-words ranking function that scores documents
by query-term frequency, weighted by inverse document frequency, and normalised
by document length. It is the canonical baseline in modern information
retrieval and the floor that any vector-search backend has to beat.

For a personal Obsidian vault, BM25 has an underrated advantage: it works
without embeddings, without a vector database, without GPU memory, and without
sending your notes to any external API. A vault with five thousand notes can be
indexed and queried in under a second on a laptop CPU.

The `naive` backend that ships with obsidian-brain-eval uses BM25 (via the
rank-bm25 package) as its default retrieval engine. It is the baseline you
score every fancier backend against. If your hybrid stack does not beat naive
BM25 on Recall@10, your hybrid stack is doing something wrong.
